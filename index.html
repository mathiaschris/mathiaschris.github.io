Master Thesis

Embodiment of a Raindrop: How Sense of Embodiment is affected in Virtual Reality

For my master's thesis, I worked on a project titled "Embodiment of a Raindrop: How Sense of Embodiment is affected in Virtual Reality". Together with my other group member, we developed a VR experience using Unity, for making an experiment about the sense of embodiment in a scenario of being a raindrop forming and falling from the sky.

In the experience, the user will start in the clouds and collect and merge water particles with their hands in order to grow bigger. After collecting enough water particles, the user is engulfed by the water and start falling towards the ground in a neighbourhood, while being able to steer in which direction they want to fall.

For the experiment of the project, four versions were developed, where there is one default version, and three other versions with one difference to the default. These differences are, having no interaction for the user, giving no haptic feedback, and detaching the water particles from the user's hands.

Tasks I personally worked with in regard to the development of the VR experience was the interaction available to the user, shaders, visual effects, haptic feedback, and the versions used for the project's experiment.

Game Programmer

DADIU - The National Academy for Digital Interactive Entertainment

In the fall of 2018, I worked as a game programmer in my team in the DADIU programme. During the this period, my team and I developed three games for android, where the two first games were developed over two weeks each, and the third game was developed over six weeks.

The first game, titled: Tilthalla, utilises the android device's sensor orientation to move the player character by tilting the device. To win the game the player has to navigate levels from start to finish, avoiding traps and enemies. My contribution to this game includes: tilt input, player movement, level system, enemy functionality, user interface, and interaction with traps.

The second game, titled: Shieldmaiden: Berserk, is a stealth game, where the player has to navigate a terrain filled with obstacles and enemies to reach the end. I programmed parts of the gameplay, the checkpoint and collectable systems, user interface, enemy behaviour, and optimisation.

The third and last game, titled: Night Painter, is a 3D side-scrolling platformer, where the player gets to paint the environment, changing the environment’s properties, and allowing the player to progress through the world. I contributed by programming gameplay, programming shaders, assisted in making in-game animations, user interface, and optimisation.

Night Painter was published on Google Play and App Store .

Study Project

Effects of Vibrotactile Feedback in Commercial Virtual Reality Systems

My 8th semester deal with investigating haptic feedback in Virtual Reality, and how it affects immersion and performance of the user. A VR application was developed, in which four different approaches in regard to haptic feedback was implemented. This includes haptic pulses from turning a phone dial, vibrations from stirring a pot of water, and rendering of vibrotactile feedback from touching a surface.

My contributions to the development of the VR application was designing and implementing the different haptic feedback approaches, programming the functionality of the application.

The paper based on this study was accepted by the ArtsIT 2018 conference and published on Springer among the post-conference proceedings.

Study Project

Tracker: Thermal Feedback Game

I investigated the use of thermal feedback in video games in my bachelor thesis. To this end, a hunting game was developed and thermal feedback was implemented as two Peltier elements in an Xbox controller. The game made use of these Peltier elements to inform the user of the target location by giving a direction. This was compared to other versions of the same game using other methods.

My contribution to the development of the game consists of programming gameplay, 3D modelling and animating some of the assets, and optimising the game’s performance.
